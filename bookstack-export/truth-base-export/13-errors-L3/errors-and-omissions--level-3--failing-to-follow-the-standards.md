Failing to Follow the Standards
This error is a result of lack of understanding of the iGUIDE Drafting Standards. Including a
space when the standards tell us not to, excluding a space when the standards don’t say to do
so, going against the standards in any way is failing to follow them.
There are special requests that go against the iGUIDE Drafting Standards but these are on a
case-by-case basis that the operator or photographer must be made aware of before
proceeding. Such examples are failure to apply Advanced Drafting processes to a “black badge”
file, failure to use appropriate window types when required or failure to add furniture in a
premium file.
This error is tricky to understand and can essentially be used as a “catch all”. It’s meant to be
used when there is gross negligence towards the standards. Take the 7’ ceiling height rule for
example, the standards clearly state the rule, however this rule was ignored on the basement
floor. Say the drafter included all 10 rooms in the basement but the entire basement is only 6’
tall. Because the drafter did not follow the drafting standards to correctly flag all rooms on the
basement floor as excluded, they will be awarded an L3 error for failing to follow iGUIDE
drafting standards.

Drafting Standard Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance for misunderstanding the Drafting Standards. Speak with your Team
Leads, Supervisors or Manager for clarifications.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L3 - Failing to follow standards. Premium furniture not placed
b. L 3 - Failing to follow standards. Property not drafted to ADS requirements
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Failed to follow drafting standards.
b. Drafting standards not followed.
You can quote the Drafting Standards as reference for the Drafters to be able to learn. Telling
them they simply didn’t follow them gives no context or information.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
