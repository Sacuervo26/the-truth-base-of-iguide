Level 3:
Drafting Spaces that Don’t Exist
Drafting Spaces that Don’t Exist or Cannot be Determined
This type of error is when a drafter assumes an area with absolutely no information. Typically
adding in rooms based on what they think should be there or adding in rooms that actually
lead to the outside.
It is at the discretion of the QA to square off minor closets such as foyer entry closets when
they have not been documented. As long as it does not affect the floor plan by more than 2%
difference in square footage.

Spaces that don’t Exist Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance. If you have to delete a room that does not exist then you must attribute
a level 3 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase
the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L3 – Insufficient data to draft storage near scan 020. No visual or laser data
b. L3 – Closet on exterior wall of bedroom at 010 cannot be drafted. No data.
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Omitted closet.
b. Omitted storage room.
c. Deleted room.

*Note: What’s the difference between “Drafting Spaces that Should not/can not be Drafted”
and this, “Drafting Spaces that Don’t Exist”? Drafting spaces that should not be drafted is
typically when operators attempt to document the space but we cannot reliably draft it. The
drafter then attempts to draft the space with limited data but it is completely unreliable.
Drafting spaces that don’t exist is when there is no attempt by the operator to document the
space but the drafter has gone ahead and drafted a completely unknown area.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
