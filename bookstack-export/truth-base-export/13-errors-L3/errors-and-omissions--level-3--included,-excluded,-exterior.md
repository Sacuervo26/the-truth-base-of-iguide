Included, Excluded, Exterior
Proper Inclusion & Exclusion of drafted spaces (Included, Excluded, Exterior).
An error regarding interior, excluded and exterior is when the incorrect type is applied. There
needs to be a thorough explanation of when and where to use each of these 3 area flags.
Interior is to be applied for a heatable, livable area. Excluded is to be used for unheated,
restricted height areas or restricted floor areas (ledges). Exterior is to be used when a space
falls outside the main property's foundation perimeter and is not livable or heated as well as
for duplicated stairs (backsplits). Exterior type also refers to and is used for any and all outdoor
spaces.
Billable Area:
Planitar billable area to operators is all drafted space with very few exceptions. It accounts for
the total drafting cost and every Planitar invoice includes a definition of the billable area.
The billable area is a sum of interior areas of all floors (including below grade) plus all drafted
spaces that may be excluded from the interior floor area totals for the purpose of property
size reporting standards, such as sunrooms, crawlspaces, any rooms attached to the house
that you can only access from outside and parts of rooms with sloped ceilings where the
height of the room is less than 5 ft. Please note that exterior wall thickness does not affect the
billable area as it only affects the exterior floor area.

Included, Excluded, Exterior Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance. If you have to change the Area Flag then you must attribute a level 3
error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally
by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L3 – incorrect area flag for void in garage scan 0022. Exterior from excluded
b. L3 – incorrect area flag for utility at 010. Ceiling <7’. Excluded from unfinished
c. L3 – Laundry at 014 included-unfinished from included. Missing ceiling.
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Wrong Area Flag.
b. Changed Area Flag.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
