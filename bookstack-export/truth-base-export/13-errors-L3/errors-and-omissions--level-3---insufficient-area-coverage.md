Insufficient Area Coverage
Insufficient Area Coverage
This type of error is when a drafter has drafted a space with little to no visual and laser data.
Drafters may incorrectly draft a missing or insufficient space based on the floor plate of a floor
above or below. Drafters may also incorrectly use their knowledge of architecture from school
to try and determine spaces with little to no data.
When there isn't sufficient visual and laser data to reliably draft the space, we cannot draft any
space where insufficient data has been provided. Anything we draft must be based on scans
taken using an iGUIDE camera system and we cannot rely on dimensions provided by any
other means (e.g. builder's floor plans, tape measurements, photos, etc.). Using the floor-tofloor alignment tool is not a means to draft areas with insufficient area coverage.

Should Not/Cannot Draft Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance. If there is a space that should not be drafted or cannot be drafted,
delete it and then you must attribute a level 3 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error
on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L3 – Cannot draft storage near 0033, no data.
b. L3 – Assume utility off garage at 020
c. L3 – Partial/unclear scan at 0019, insufficient data to draft area.
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Removed storage room.
b. Deleted room.
c. Can’t draft the storage.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
