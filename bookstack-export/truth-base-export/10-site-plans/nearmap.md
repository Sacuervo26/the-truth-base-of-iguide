Site Plans – Nearmap
Overview
Nearmap is a company that provides high-resolution aerial imagery and location intelligence through its
software and online services. They offer up-to-date aerial maps, including vertical and oblique views, for
urban and regional areas in the United States, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.
The Nearmap MapBrowser application is the primary tool used by the Drafting Team to initiate every Site
Plan project. From MapBrowser, drafters must export a high-quality aerial image of the respective lot to
serve as a visual underlay. This image acts as the essential reference for defining property boundaries
and accurately placing landscaping elements within the Draft software.

Getting Started
Every person working on Site Plans must have a Nearmap account beforehand. Once the account has
been provided and initiated, please navigate to nearmap.com. Locate the Log in button at the top
navigation bar. Under Log in, select the MapBrowser option and enter your credentials.

Orienting & Refining the View
Before exporting aerial images there are some steps that need to be done beforehand:
1. Every property needs to be rotated so that the front of the property faces “UP” (portrait
orientation)
a. To rotate the map, use the bottom-right Compass Tool.
b. If the property sits at an angle to the street, rotate until the building itself aligns
vertically on your screen.
c. Micro-rotational adjustments can be made later in Draft
2. Pan and zoom so the entire lot area is visible.
3. Use the Timeline at the bottom-center of the screen to look for the most suitable image.
a. Aerial images are meant to provide a visual reference of the property’s surroundings.
Consequentially, drafters need to look for the image that provides as much information
as possible.
b. Avoid images where trees or other features cover the layout of the lot.

Exporting the Image
After the image has been oriented and refined, please click on the Export bottom located in the left tool
bar (Figure #3). An EXPORT section will appear on the left side of the screen with all the possible
settings that can be changed. Additionally, a white box will appear enclosing the boundaries of the
resulting image. We will refer to this box as the Area of Interest (AOI). First, ensure the following settings
are selected:
1. Export Type: Basic
2. Resolution (GSD): 3 in (Highest)
