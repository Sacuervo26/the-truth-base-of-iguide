Room Types: Residential
Room Types
Once a property is drafted every area must be assigned a room type and a room name.
Generally a room label should not be repeated in a property (aside from bathrooms and
bedrooms). The most common room labels are assigned a shortcut number. Each room label
has an associated room type (eg. Closet is a storage type, Bedroom is a bedroom type).
To assign a room label:
1. Press “R” to enter room mode.
2. “Double left-click” in each bounded room to open the room name menu.
3. Begin typing a room name to activate the drop down menu.
4. Choose the correct room from the options listed.
If naming multiple rooms with identical labels:
1. Ctrl+ left click to highlight all applicable rooms, highlighted rooms will appear in yellow.
2. Press one of the 10 room shortcuts to choose a room label.
Note: The double click method does NOT work for naming multiple rooms
If a room is being named something not on the auto select list ensure that:
1. The room name is being displayed by “right-clicking” the area and selecting
label/arrows > Show label.
2. The room type is accurate, this can be adjusted by “right-clicking” and selecting room
type and choosing the most applicable type from

4. The room name is in all capital letters and spelled correctly.

Adjustment of Labels
After all the rooms are labeled, sometimes the labels are placed overlapping the scans or other
important objects.
To fix this: in R mode right-click > Auto-place ALL room labels. This will move the label so it is
no longer overlapping the scan.
●

Labels must be placed horizontally and vertically (only if it can’t fit horizontally).

●

After auto placing labels, some labels will still be overlapping objects or rotated
improperly, and must be manually adjusted.

To manually adjust a label press “R” until the interfering scan is prominent, then, hover the
cursor over the label and left-click and drag it to another position.
●

Right-click on the label and the font size can be changed if necessary.

●

There is also a line and dot overtop the label which allows you to rotate the label (only
do this if absolutely necessary).

●

Dimensions and areas can be hidden to reduce label size.

Most Common Room Types
Bedroom
●

A room for sleeping in.

●

Can be assigned to any number of rooms.

●

Must contain a window.

●

May contain a closet or have enough space to fit a wardrobe.

●

Will often be decorated with a bed but may appear as an office.

Primary
●

Can only be assigned to one room per property.

●

Will usually be the largest bedroom in the property often with an ensuite bathroom and
large closet.

●

Some properties may not have a designated Primary bedroom so all bedrooms can
remain labelled as “Bedroom.”

●

Some properties may only contain one bedroom so the single bedroom can remain
labelled as "Bedroom."

Living
●

A room within the property that is for general and casual everyday use.

●

The main living area in the property, often attached to or split with the dining room.

●

Typically placed immediately after the foyer within homes.

4PC Bath ( 4 piece bath)
●

Can be assigned to any number of rooms.

●

The room must not be an ensuite bathroom and must contain exactly 4 pieces.

●

A piece is defined as a source of water eg. Toilet, sink, faucet, shower head, bidet.

●

A common 4 piece bathroom will contain 1 sink, 1 toilet and one bathtub (containing
both a faucet and showerhead) counting as 2 pieces.

Foyer
●

The entrance of the property that usually connects other various rooms.

●

Only assigned to one area per property.

●

Used to designate the entry area to the property.

●

If there is no defined area a manual label can be used.
4

Kitchen
●

A room or area where food is prepared or cooked.

●

Often will only contain 1 per property but may have a second commonly found in the
basement.

●

Area must contain common cooking fixtures including sinks, fridges or ovens.

Dining
●

An area within the household where meals are eaten.

●

Only assigned to one area per property.

●

Large area with a dining table.

●

Any smaller dining area can be called “Breakfast.”

Closet
●

A tall recess or wardrobe with a door and acts as storage.

●

A room or small storage area attached to any room.

Garage
●

A space used to house a motor vehicle or vehicles that may be attached to or detached
from the home.

Void
●

Similar to the structural voids.

●

Used for any unusable space.

●

Can be flagged as “Excluded” when the void is inside the foundation perimeter or
flagged as “Exterior” when the void is outside the foundation perimeter.

Other Common Room Types
Attic
●

An attic is a space found directly below the pitched roof of a home.

●

Attics can be flagged as unfinished when visible construction materials are present.

Balcony
●

A platform that is typically enclosed by a railing located on the exterior of a building.
Typically has access from a common room or bedroom.

Bar
●

Similar to a kitchen, usually with no stove but contains a sink, wine fridge, standard
fridge & dishwasher.

●

Used for serving alcoholic drinks at home.

Barn
●

A large building used for storing livestock (horses, cows, pigs) hay or straw.

Carport
●

A carport is a shelter with limited protection for a car consisting of a roof supported on
posts or columns. It can be built beside or attached to the home (free standing or
attached to an exterior wall).

●

The carport is not the driveway.

Cold Room
●

Room in the basement that is unfinished and usually below the front porch on the floor
above.

●

It is not an insulated space and since it is below grade it remains cool all year round.

Courtyard
●

An area that does not contain a roof but is either completely or almost completely
enclosed by the walls of a building.

●

Often contains walkways and gardens.

6

Deck
●

A structure that may or may not be directly attached to the home made up of wood
planks or plates.

●

Most commonly seen in backyards accessed via a sliding door.

Den
●

Smaller room on any floor, usually big enough for just 1-2 people.

●

Can be an alternative name for a bedroom without a window.

Elevator
●

A moving platform or compartment that is contained within a shaft for raising or
lowering people or items from one floor to another.

Ensuite
●

A bathroom that is only accessible from a single bedroom.

●

Number of pieces is noted (3PC Ensuite).

Family
●

A space that is used by all family members, typically for relaxation or recreation.

●

Used for a second living area on a floor when Living has already been used.

●

Often contains a fireplace.

Great Room
●

These rooms typically have grand raised ceilings.

●

A large multi-use room, combining the roles of several other traditional rooms like the
living room, family room and office into one large space.

●

Usually these spaces are centralized within the home.

Gym
●

A room containing exercise equipment (yoga matts, dumbbells, treadmills).

●

A room used to perform physical exercise.

Hall
●

Rooms in between other major rooms.

●

May often be odd shapes.

Laundry
●

A room used for washing clothes.

●

Contains a washing machine/dryer or necessary hookup for them.

●

Often it contains a sink.

●

May appear as a normal closet, look for hot water and cold water hookups.

Loft
●

This space is typically a building's upper storey or an elevated area within a room
(mezzanine, platform).

●

Also can be used as a storage space underneath the roof which is typically accessed via
a ladder.

Mudroom
●

Usually a separate entrance to the house, sometimes includes the door to the garage.

Office
●

Used as a place for commercial, professional or bureaucratic work.

●

Similar to a bedroom and may not contain a closet.

●

May contain a window on an interior wall.

Patio
●

A paved or tiled outdoor area that abuts or is adjoining the house. Not typically made
with asphalt ( do not get this mistaken with driveways).
8

Porch
●

A covered area outside an entry to the home typically made with concrete or wood.

●

May wrap around a significant portion of the front of a home.

Rec Room
●

A large room typically located in the basement used for entertainment and recreation.

Servery
●

A small room usually between the kitchen and dining.

●

Has countertops and sometimes an extra sink.

Stairs
●

All sections of stairs.

Storage
●

Any room that is unused or used for extra storage.

●

A larger closet that may be used for longer term storage than a closet.

Sun Room
●

A room usually around the outside of a floor with many windows and sometimes
another door to outside.

●

May or may not be included in the total above grade square footage, based on if it's
heated.
○

A specific room label of “SUN ROOM (exterior)” will be used in cases where the
area is not heated.

Theater
●

Home entertainment rooms used for viewing movies or shows.

●

These spaces reproduce the movie theater experience at home.

Utility
●

Contains furnace, water heater, electrical type machinery for the property.
