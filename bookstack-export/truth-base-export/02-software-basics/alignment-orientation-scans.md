Alignment and Orientation of Scans 
Correctly aligning and orienting scans is critical to ensure that what’s drafted is an accurate 
representation of a property.  
 
• 
Before aligning individual scans, it is important to make sure the Main Floor is 
correctly oriented: 
o Press “F” to enter Floor Mode (F-Mode) 
o Make sure the main entrance of the property is facing to the right or 
downwards while the floor is in portrait orientation. 
• 
In Floor Mode press 2 for Draft to auto-align all the scans (skip this step in very big 
files). After, still in F-Mode, press 1 multiple times until the floor is fully straightened. 
• 
Press “M” to enter movement mode (M mode). 
• 
Press and hold “S” (Select Mode). Then, left click and drag the mouse to select all the 
scans at once. Press 1 to individually straightened each scan. 
• 
Press and use the “spacebar” to switch between scans. 
• 
Select a scan as the beginning scan to base all scan alignment from. This scan 
should not be moved once initially aligned. A scan with laser data registering on 
walls and other objects common to other laser data would be a good choice for a 
starting scan. In residential properties it is suggested to pick a scan that is in one 
corner of the property.  
• 
Once a scan is selected, press “1” on the upper left of the keyboard to auto rotate 
the scan. Ensure the wall readings in the scan are oriented as close to vertical and 
horizontal as possible. 
• 
To rotate a scan right-click and drag. “Alt” can be used to overlap the snap-to-grid 
only when required. 
• 
Move to the next closest scan which shares as many wall readings as the previous 
scan. See figure 1. 
• 
Align this scan and move it so that the readings from identical walls are overlapped. 
• 
Repeat this process for all scans on this floor.

Dealing With Misleading Data 
Occasionally scans contain double lines. To align these scans properly, find a middle ground 
between the 2 (or double) lines and use that as the actual position for a point on the laser 
data.  
 
Additionally the user can right-click on a single scan that contains double lines and choose the 
option, Fix Laser Data. This will remove extra data (not always beneficial as it may take away 
too much data) and will only be applied during the current session. Once the draft is re-
opened the additional laser data will appear.
