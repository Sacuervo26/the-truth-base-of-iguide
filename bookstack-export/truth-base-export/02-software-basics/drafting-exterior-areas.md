Drafting Exterior Areas
Exterior Areas
Operators will often include scans outside of the property to give additional information about
exterior features to the home. To be able to draft exterior areas, scans need to be taken on top
or over the area of interest. Exterior areas are always a visual representation and do not
include dimensions, nor are these areas included in the total square footage of a property.
Exterior areas are drafted through the following process:
1. Set the floor for the area by changing the camera height. Press “T” to enter measure
mode. Then using “Shift + Alt + Middle Mouse Button”, select an area where the floor or
ground intersects the exterior wall.
2. Using the D6 Visible Divider Line Type, trace the perimeter of the exterior area of
interest.
3. Make sure the D6 Visible Divider Line Type is connected to a building. Please keep in
mind that the D6 line behaves the same as the D3 line.
4. Label the area appropriately (such as patio, balcony, deck)
a. Labeling the space as such will automatically assign the Room Type as
“Outdoor/protrusion/duplicated”.
5. Draw any exterior columns if applicable. Press “O” to enter the Object mode. Use the
left drop-down menu to access the “Structural” objects. Find the column objects that
best fit the scenario.
6. Draw any exterior stairs/ramps/steps leading to or from an outdoor structure. Use the
D7 line to draw these features. No D6 or D3 is necessary to limit these areas.

Common Exterior Areas
Balcony
●

A platform that is typically enclosed by a railing located on the exterior of a building.
Typically has access from a common room or bedroom.

Carport
●

A carport is a shelter with limited protection for a car consisting of a roof supported on
posts or columns. It can be built beside or attached to the home (free standing or
attached to an exterior wall).

●

The carport is not the driveway.

Courtyard
●

An area that does not contain a roof but is either completely or almost completely
enclosed by the walls of a building.

●

Often contains walkways and gardens.

Deck
●

A structure that may or may not be directly attached to the home made up of wood
planks or plates.

●

Most commonly seen in backyards accessed via a sliding door.

Patio
●

A paved or tiled outdoor area that abuts or adjoins the house. Not typically made with
asphalt (do not get this mistaken with driveways).

Porch
●

A covered area outside an entry to the home typically made with concrete or wood.

●

May wrap around a significant portion of the front of a home.
