Drafting Straight Stairs
Straight stairs are a type of stairs that do not change in direction. They are one of the most
common types of stairs used in residential and commercial properties. Some flights of stairs
are so long that they will have a central landing, especially in commercial buildings.
To draft a set of straight stairs follow the instructions below:
●

Press “D” to enter draw mode.

●

Press “7” to use structurelines.

●

Draw a structure line following the centerline of the interior walls and at the start and
end point of stairs.
○

The start of the stair is either at the first riser (stairs up) or at the edge of the first
tread (stairs down)

○

The end of the stairs is either at the last riser (stairs up) or at the edge of the
final tread (stairs down).

●

Right-click on the structure line at the start of the stair segment.

●

Hover over Generate Stairs and select either up or down depending on the direction
of stairs.

Drafting Stairs in a Garage
Structural or permanent stairs in a garage are to be drafted and bound with a D3 Invisible
Divider Line Type. These stairs should be labelled as Stairs UP or DN based on the floor level.
The most important part when drafting structural or permanent stairs in a garage is to set the
area flag to the same as the garage, “Exterior (uninhabitable detached structure or outside
foundation perimeter).”

Stairs leading from inside the home into the garage are a common feature. These stairs can be
few in number, non-permanent, or built using exposed plywood and will be depicted in Draft
for barrier-free purposes. Stairs will be drafted with a D7 Structural Line Type and will remain
unbounded, so as to not detract from the total area of the garage. Additionally, D4 Half Wall
Line Type or D5 Railing Line Type will be used to draft half walls or railings if they are present.
Stair direction leaders and gates will also be omitted when drafting non-permanent stairs in a
garage.
