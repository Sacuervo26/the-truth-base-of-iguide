Drafting Spiral Stairs
Spiral Stairs
Spiral staircases are a space saving feature for residential or commercial applications. Spiral
staircases do not take up a lot of room and can be used in smaller spaces. These types of
staircases also have a central support.
To draft a spiral staircase follow the steps below:
1. Determine the diameter of the spiral staircase and draw a wall segment (D2 or D5).
2. Place a break in the middle of the line by double clicking the segment.
Note: If the line isn't divided equally the draft program will make each side equal in width
when generating the staircase.
3. Right click on one of the 2 line segments, scroll down to Generate stairs and pick a
direction.
4. Once the stairs are generated, highlight everything to flip the direction of the directional
arrow using the “V” vertical flip or “H” horizontal flip.
5. Use an invisible divider and wall type line to divide the circle of the spiral stairs and
remove unwanted parts. Spiral stairs can be unidirectional or bidirectional.
6. Place a circular structural object in the center if a central post exists.
