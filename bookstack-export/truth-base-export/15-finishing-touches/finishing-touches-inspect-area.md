Inspect Area  
It’s important to use the Inspect Area button in Draft to confirm instances such as correct 
perimeter wall routing, the bounding box of the floor plan, inclusion of spaces, exclusion of 
spaces whether it’s inside the foundation perimeter or outside the foundation perimeter.  
 
 
Figure 1 - Inspect Area Button 
 
Pressing the yellow Inspect Area button will highlight the areas on each floor that are either 
excluded or outside the foundation perimeter.  
Perimeter Wall Routing  
 
The Perimeter Wall Routing boundary is the red line in the middle of the exterior perimeter 
walls for the purpose of calculating an Exterior Area. 
 
The Exterior Area given on the iGUIDE report is the footprint of the building or the amount of 
area that the building takes up. The iGUIDE camera system measures the Interior Area of the 
building. From this Interior Area, a perimeter can be established for the floor. A measured 
exterior wall thickness combined with the interior perimeter measurement will determine the 
Exterior Area of the floor.  
 
Note: The footprint of the exterior walls of the attached garage will not be included in the total 
exterior area. The attached garage will not be highlighted since the attached garage is not a 
billable area.

Bounding Box 
 
There are two bounding boxes also known as viewports or bounding rectangles.  
 
The Fuchsia dashed bounding box indicates the furthest extents of the floor plan for the sake 
of the Virtual Tour or on the Portal. This takes into account the furthest scan locations.  
 
The Green dashed bounding box indicates the furthest extents of the draftable areas for the 
sake of the iGUIDE pdfs. This does not take into account the furthest scan locations.

Inclusion of Spaces 
 
After hitting the Inspect Area button, areas highlighted in yellow are marked as included areas 
which contribute to the Total Interior Area of the property.  
 
 
Figure 4 - Included Spaces (Highlighted in Yellow)

Exclusion of Spaces  
 
After hitting the Inspect Area button, areas highlighted in blue are marked as excluded areas 
which do not contribute to the Total Interior Area. Instead, these spaces are added to the Total 
Exterior Area of the property.  
 
Areas such as Garages, Ledges, Bay Windows and Open to Below areas will be automatically 
excluded from the livable area.  
 
Areas such as sunrooms and certain foyers or entry rooms that do not meet the requirements 
for year-round use need to be excluded from the livable area. 
 
● To manually exclude areas, follow these steps: 
○ Press “R” to enter room mode 
○ Right-click the room area you wish to exclude Room type > Change Area Flags 
> Excluded (inside foundation perimeter). 
● These steps can also be done to manually include room areas. 
 
After hitting the Inspect Area button, areas that are not highlighted such are considered 
“Exterior” spaces and will not contribute to any area totals.
