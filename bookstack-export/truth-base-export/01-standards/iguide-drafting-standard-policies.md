iGUIDE Drafting Standard & Policies (rev. 1.5.0)

Introduction
The purpose of this iGUIDE Drafting Standard is to outline a set of rules, requirements, and
guidelines necessary for reliable drafting of iGUIDE floor plans using measurement data
captured by an iGUIDE camera system. It includes requirements and guidelines that iGUIDE
operators must be aware of and follow to successfully provide information to the Planitar
Drafting Team. These rules and requirements are intended to provide a clear and consistent
system for providing data.
Square footage reporting is based on the ANSI Z765-2013 Standard, Alberta Residential
Measurement Standard (RMS), and elements of the International Property Measurement
Standard (IPMS).

Key iGUIDE Definitions
Area: Exterior and Interior
The Exterior Area given on the iGUIDE report is the footprint of the building or the amount
of area that the building takes up. The iGUIDE camera system measures the Interior Area of
the building. From this Interior Area, a perimeter can be established for the floor. A
measured Perimeter Wall Thickness combined with the Interior Area will determine the
Exterior Area of the floor.

Area: Excluded and Included
Floor area calculations can either include or exclude certain areas. Excluded areas are not
included in area calculations and are therefore not represented in the resulting area totals.

Grade: Above and Below
Grade refers to the level of the ground around the exterior of the property. Levels that have
any part below the grade may be considered in some regions to be completely below-grade
levels. In other regions a level may be considered below grade only if a certain minimum
fraction of it is below grade.

Floor Areas
1. iGUIDE floor plans are drafted to be a schematic representation of the home. They
are not meant to be architectural floor plans detailing every aspect of the home.
Please refer to the Appendix of this document.
2. Below-grade levels are not included in the Total Above Grade Floor Area. You can
indicate to Planitar if a level is below or above grade by marking the “Below Grade” or
“Above Grade” checkboxes on the applicable floors in Stitch. In the case of
separating one floor into multiple floors, the Planitar Drafting Team will use the grade
status set in Stitch for the original floor.
3. The Exterior Area will not be reported if no exterior Perimeter Wall Thickness is
specified. Consequently, Finished/Unfinished Area will not be reported.
4. Areas included in floor area calculations are colored on the floor plans. All excluded
areas are shown white. Some colored areas may not be included in all area
calculations (e.g. below grade areas). Planitar billable area to operators is all drafted
space with very few exceptions. Please refer to the iGUIDE Knowledge Base
article, ”Billing FAQ & Billable Area Defined.”
5. Total Exterior Area is the sum of all Exterior Areas per building. Please refer to the
iGUIDE Knowledge Base article, “iGUIDE Method of Measurement.”
6. Additions to the main building are included as part of the main building if they meet
the following requirements:
●
The area must be able to be heated to 72F/22C year round. The heating
method must not be an electric heater plugged into the outlet. Acceptable
heat sources include: fireplaces, baseboard heaters and central heating ducts;
●
The area in question must be waterproof;
●
The addition must have a foundation.
7. For proper area reporting, floors belonging to separate buildings must be assigned
to a new building in Stitch. It is the operator’s responsibility to assign floors to the
appropriate buildings using Stitch.
8. All rooms must have a minimum floor to ceiling height of 7ft/2.1m. Rooms with
sloped ceilings are measured at a height of 5ft/1.5m only if the ceiling height exceeds
7ft/2.1m in at least one location.

9. Extensions from the main property, such as bay and bow windows, are included in
the total floor area only if they have a minimum floor to ceiling height of 5ft/1.5m
and have a walkable floor with the rest of the room.
10. Attics that do not have permanent stairs will not be included in the total floor area.
11. Areas such as attached garages, cold cellars, outdoor spaces, and sunrooms not
suitable for year-round use are excluded from the Interior Area by default for
Residential iGUIDEs.
12. For Residential properties, any area of the main building that cannot be accessed
directly through the inside of the building or through an attached garage will be
excluded from the Total Interior Area of the building. For example, a room that is
only accessible from an exterior door but is still attached to the main building would
be excluded from the Total Interior Area.
13. Excluded or included areas can be specified. For Excluded spaces, operators can
use the “Add notes” tool in Stitch and place a note saying “Excluded” on the floor map
inside that room. The excluded spaces will be excluded from any type of reported
area (interior, exterior, finished, unfinished), e.g. placing an “Excluded” label over a
sunroom that is not suitable for year-round use will notify the Drafting Team to
exclude that area.
14. Unfinished or finished areas can be specified. For Unfinished spaces, operators can
use the “Add note” tool in Stitch and place a note saying “Unfinished” on the floor
map inside that room, e.g. placing an “unfinished” label within an unfinished attic, the
Drafting Team will then mark the room as Unfinished in the iGUIDE for proper
reporting. This is similar to placing a note saying “Excluded” to mark excluded
spaces, with the difference being that excluded spaces are always excluded from
any type of reported floor area (interior, exterior, finished, and unfinished). If you
forget to mark a room as Unfinished in Stitch, you will be able to do so in the iGUIDE
Editor on the Portal once the iGUIDE is published.
15. Stairs are included in the floor area of the floor from which the stairs descend.
16. Apartments, townhouses, duplexes and other dwellings with common walls in
some regions may not require the Total Exterior Area to be reported. To indicate to
Planitar that no Total Exterior Area calculation and reporting should be performed, do
not measure or provide a Perimeter Wall Thickness.

17. Common amenity and shared areas will be drafted by default for AEC, IRC, and CRE
industry iGUIDEs. This includes, but is not limited to, areas such as rec rooms, pools,
gyms, and common area hallways in apartment buildings, condo/townhouse
complexes, shopping malls, plazas, etc. For RRE industry iGUIDEs, these areas will
not be drafted in the iGUIDE unless otherwise noted by the operator.

Labels and Room Ordering
18. Dimensions of the major rooms will be shown on the floor plan but dimensions for
minor rooms will be hidden. Minor rooms include foyers, closets, hallways etc.
Space limitations on the floor plan prevent this information from being easily read but
it can be activated on the Portal.
19. The Drafting Team will assign room labels using their best judgment unless
otherwise instructed by the operator and with very few exceptions. Once the
iGUIDE is published, iGUIDE editors can modify labels and add custom labels through
the Portal.
20. The Drafting Team will not modify the order of scans on a floor. All such
modifications must be done in Stitch prior to submitting the data to Planitar.
21. The Drafting Team will select an initial starting panorama using their best
judgment if an initial panorama is not already selected in Stitch. Once the iGUIDE is
published, iGUIDE editors can modify the initial panorama on the Portal.

Data Collection
22. All spaces intended to be drafted must be scanned with an iGUIDE camera system.
Scans must consist of visual and laser data that is adequate for proper drafting.
Scans in areas that are not to be shown in the iGUIDE can be disabled in Stitch and
on the Portal.
23. All doors, windows, and stairs must be clearly visible in panoramas so that they
can be correctly sized and positioned on floor plans. That requires creating at least
one scan in every space that has doors, windows, or stairs. Larger spaces, such as
open concept kitchens, living rooms, and primary bedrooms often require two or
more scans.
24. Scans are required within connecting spaces such as hallways and doorways
between rooms in order to allow for reliable alignment.

25. Small closets which cannot be entered by an iGUIDE camera system must be
scanned through an opened door if the dimensions cannot be gauged from the
surrounding walls.
26. Premium iGUIDEs must have panoramas clearly showing countertops and other
fixtures so that they can be properly placed on the detailed floor plans. This can
include built-in countertops, built-in cupboards, built-in cabinetry, refrigerators,
under-counter dishwashers, sinks, bathtubs, showers, laundry washers and dryers,
etc.
●
Premium objects are guaranteed to be added in rooms such as kitchens, bars,
bathrooms and laundry rooms (excluding furniture) for Residential iGUIDEs.
●
Premium objects are guaranteed to be added in rooms such as kitchens, bars,
washrooms, laundry facilities, retail stores (store shelving) and offices
(cubicles) for Commercial iGUIDEs. Premium objects and countertops will also
be added in other rooms not listed above, provided they are permanent builtin fixtures (excluding furniture).
●
Premium details will be included for outdoor areas adjacent to the subject
building.
27. Premium iGUIDEs will only feature Furniture for properties that are clean and/or
staged. Unfurnished, disorganized or damaged properties will not have furniture
included in the iGUIDE. Premium iGUIDEs must have panoramas clearly showing
furniture and other objects, highlighting the arrangement of items such as sofas,
chairs, tables, beds, and cabinets.
●
Premium furniture will be featured in key rooms such as living rooms,
bedrooms, dining areas, and home offices for Residential iGUIDEs.
●
For Commercial iGUIDEs, Premium furniture will be included for office
buildings, retail stores and restaurants for spaces like conference rooms,
lounges, offices, and retail areas.
●
Additional furniture may be added to other rooms if the space is staged or
furnished in a way that showcases the room’s layout and functionality.
28. Residential iGUIDEs with partially documented floors missing major rooms will be
pre-published. Residential iGUIDEs with partially documented floors missing minor
rooms will be published. Residential iGUIDEs with partially documented floors can be
published per operator instructions. The operator can return to the property to collect
any missing spaces for a pre-published iGUIDE, to then create a Draft Update to
complete the floor plan. Commercial iGUIDE floor plans will not be pre-published,
instead they will always be published.

29. For outdoor structures to be drafted, scans of the area must consist of adequate
visual and laser data in order to determine the boundary. Where posts or railings are
present, it is recommended to lower the iGUIDE camera system to a height below the
top of the posts or railings. Steps, stairs, ramps, and transitions leading to or from an
outdoor structure will also be represented in the iGUIDE. Columns captured in these
areas will also be represented.
30. The iGUIDE Drafting Team produces iGUIDE floor plans, not site plans. If sufficient
data is available, the iGUIDE can feature built outdoor structures, whether part of
complete iGUIDE floor plan or independently, that adjoin or abut the subject building
(e.g. decks, balconies, patios, carports, porches), not yards or other landscaping
(enclosed by fencing or otherwise).
31. Exterior scans that are within the laser measurement range from a property can
benefit from having the laser turned on for better positioning in Stitch.
32. All scans must be sufficiently aligned in Stitch on each floor in order for an iGUIDE
to be processed.
33. Exterior scans must be positioned in their desired locations in Stitch. The Planitar
Drafting Team will move exterior scans that capture the target property, including
perimeter walls and adjacent outdoor structures (decks, patios, porches), within an
acceptable range to reliably draft these structures and adjust the cosmetic thickness
of the perimeter walls. Exterior scans positioned too far from the property will not be
adjusted, ensuring that only scans within the desired range are aligned.
34. Floor plans will be drafted so that the front door is facing the bottom or right hand
side of the iGUIDE Viewer. This ensures the front of the home will be facing the
bottom, right or left hand side of the PDF floor plans.
35. Rooms on floor plans are drafted and labeled based on what is currently shown in
the scan. Rooms on floor plans are not drafted based on past or future configuration
(eg. an office showroom that will eventually be converted to a garage will be labeled
and shown as an office). Rooms on the floor plan can be labeled based on past or
future configuration only per operator request.

Stray Subjects in Panos
36. It is the operator's responsibility to edit people, pets and reflections in mirrors out
of panoramas and Planitar does not provide image-processing services. There is a
two-week grace period after the iGUIDE is created during which operators can
replace panoramas in the iGUIDE with edited versions on the iGUIDE Portal.

Rooms Without Any Perpendicular Walls
37. The iGUIDE camera system must be leveled and the tripod height must be adjusted
so that the laser scanner height above the floor is at 5ft/1.5m.

Back-splits and Side-splits
38. Floors in split-level homes with a vertical separation of greater than 4ft/1.2m or
which have 7 or more steps of stairs should be considered different levels. It is
suggested that operators create a new floor in Survey or Stitch for each level with a
vertical separation of greater than 4ft/1.2m or which have 7 or more steps of stairs.

Elevators
39. An elevator will only be included towards the Interior Area on the lowest level of a
commercial property. Only the floor containing the elevator pit will count towards
the floor area as the elevator cab does not count towards the floor area for each level
above.
40. An elevator will be included towards the Interior Area on all levels of a residential
property. An elevator shaft, laundry chute, and/or a dumbwaiter will be included
towards the Interior Area on all levels of a residential property.

Delivery Time and Requirements
1. iGUIDEs that have all required data uploaded to the Portal will be processed within
24 hours.
2. Exact completion time is contingent on the daily volume of iGUIDEs.
3. iGUIDEs are drafted in the order in which the files are uploaded to the Portal.
4. No drafting occurs on official holidays in Ontario, Canada.
5. For iGUIDEs greater than 10,000 sq ft, standard turnaround time for the completed
iGUIDE is not guaranteed. The delivery date will be determined by the size and
complexity of the property.
Any questions must be addressed by opening a ticket at support.youriguide.com

The Floor Plan

1. Title: The iGUIDE title, this is usually the address of the iGUIDE or the name of the
business.
2. Areas: Total Interior Area, Exterior Area and Excluded Area values
3. Compass Rotation: The camera system has a compass. When panos are shot the
compass rotation value is given to that pano. The floor will have the combined compass
value of all the panos on that floor.
4. Date: When the iGUIDE was completed and uploaded to the Portal.
5. Scale: The full length of the bar represents 2m when overlayed on the Floor Plan.
6. Door Object: This is an example of a standard door.
7. Window Object: This is what all windows look like, with varying lengths.
8. Stairs: This is an example of a staircase. All stairs include an “UP” or “DN” label to indicate
if they go up or down.
9. Dimension arrows: These arrows show where the outer limits are for the dimensions.
10. Premium Objects: This is an example of an object that gets added to the floor plans for
premium iGUIDES.
11. Wall Break: This wall doesn’t actually exist on the floor plan. It is there to represent a
separation between the stairs going up and the stairs going down.
12. Fireplaces: The drawn box shows the outline of the fireplace accompanied by a “F/P”
label.
13. Label, Dimension and Area: These labels give details about the room itself and can be
turned on/off on the Portal.
14. Void Spaces: These represent areas that cannot be occupied or used. Typically contain
piping or structural members of a home.

Wall & Line Types
1. Exterior Wall Type: Can be bigger or smaller depending on the exterior wall thickness
entered Survey/ Stitch.
2. Interior Wall Type: Typically, 12 cm, but can vary depending on the construction of the
home.
3. Invisible Divider Line Type: Represents the open division between spaces that aren’t
separated by walls.
4. Half Wall Type: Walls that only extend partway from floor to ceiling.
5. Railing Wall Type: Used for wood or metal railings.
6. Basic Line Type: Represents 3 different line types.
a. Construction Line: Used for fireplace hearths, stairs and changes in elevation.
b. Outdoor Divider Line: Used to draw decks, balconies, patios, porches, etc.
c. Object Line: Used to draw countertops and other objects for Premium iGUIDEs.
