Fireplaces
How to Draft Fireplaces
Fireplace hearths will be drafted using a D7 Structural Line Type (treating it similarly to
a step) and the fireplace protrusion will be drafted with a D9 Cosmetic Wall Type to
allow for tagging.
Draw Fireplaces using the following:
1. Draw the hearth (if raised) using a D7 Structural Line Type.
2. Draw the protrusion of the fireplace from the wall using a D9 Cosmetic Wall
Type.
a. If there is no protrusion, just use an “F/P” label.
● Fireplace: A place for a domestic fire, especially a grate or hearth at the base of
a chimney.
● Hearth: A hearth is the place in a home where a fire is or was traditionally kept
for home heating and for cooking, usually constituted by at least a horizontal
hearthstone and often enclosed to varying degrees by any combination of
reredos, fireplace, oven, smoke hood, or chimney. Other definitions include:
1) The floor of a fireplace.
2) The area in front of a fireplace.
3) A brick, stone, or concrete area in front of a fireplace.
● Hearthstone: A flat stone forming a hearth or part of a hearth.
● Fireplace Mantel: The fireplace mantel or mantelpiece acts as a hood that is
projected over a fire grate to catch the smoke. The term mantel has changed to
include the decorative framing around the fireplace.
Note: Fireplaces are included within the room they exist in but if it is against a ledge or
forms part of a ledge, the ledge will take precedence.
