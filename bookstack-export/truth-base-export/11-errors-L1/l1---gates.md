Stair Gate Errors & Omissions
A stair gate error is when the gate has been improperly paired, improperly placed or the gates
have been neglected to be paired. Additionally, a stair gate error is when a deactivated scan
has been activated by the drafter to allow the stair gate to turn green.

Gate Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance for stair gates. Gates must be placed and remain on the stairs. An
improper pairing or moving the gate far away from the stairs counts as 1 L1 error.
*Note: It’ll be on the QA to make a judgment call to move the gate slightly to try and pick up an
enabled scan, however this CANNOT be marked as an error towards a drafter as the
instruction is to leave the gate on the stair.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L1 - Gate 1 incorrectly paired at scan 0004
b. L1 - Incorrectly paired gates on stairs down @ 0020
c. L1 – Gate placement incorrect at 0010
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Wrong gate pairing.
b. Gates mismatched.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several miniscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
