Level 1:

Missing a Single Step/Transition Between Rooms/Floors
Step/Transition Errors
The type of error is when a single/double/triple step or transition has been drawn incorrectly
or missed entirely. This error is separate from stairs as a single transition, a two step transition
or 3 step transition is included within the room it’s contained in. This means for
Single/Double/Triple steps, they do not contain a directional arrow, UP/DN label or sectioned
off as a separate space with a D3 line.
For garage stairs, which lead from the main floor into the garage itself, the steps are drawn
only. The directional arrow and label needs to be removed and the space should not be bound
with a D3 line.

Step/Transition Error Tolerances
Single/Double/Triple steps or transitions will have an error tolerance of 5cm/2” in terms of its
starting point and end point. If you have to adjust the starting or end point of the steps by
anything greater than 5cm/2” then you must attribute a level 1 error towards the Drafter. Note
down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L1 - cosmetic stairs not drafted in garage at scan 010
b. L1 - incorrect direction leader for cosmetic stairs at scan 0012
c. L1 - incorrect direction label for stairs at scan 020
d. L1 - Missed set of stairs near scan 0030
e. L1 - Starting location of stairs at scan 004 incorrect. Adjusted by more than 2”
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Missing steps.
b. Missed transition.
c. Steps drawn incorrectly.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
