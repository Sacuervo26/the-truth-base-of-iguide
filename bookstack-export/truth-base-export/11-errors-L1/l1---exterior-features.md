Missing/Improper Exterior Features (Decks, Patios, Porches)
Exterior Feature Errors & Omissions
Errors regarding exterior spaces are attributed to omitting areas or features when sufficient
data is provided.
The most common exterior feature error is omission. Simply forgetting to draft anything
outside when sufficient data has been provided.

Below is a common example of exterior stairs. Drafters should be drafting stairs where they
appear and including a direction label and arrow. If the stairs have less than 3 steps, the label
and arrow can be omitted. Stairs are not to be enclosed with D3 lines or have a room label
associated with them,
The direction of the label and arrow can be determined by how someone might use the stairs
if they were touring through the home starting at the main door of entry. For example, during
a tour, one would enter the home through the front door to access the Foyer. If the exterior
stairs leading to the front door are ascending (going up), the direction will be up. Similarly,
when touring the home, exterior areas that are not attached to the main entry door are
accessed from inside the home. For example, a back yard deck is accessed from inside the
home with stairs descending (going down) to the yard, the direction will be down.

Exterior Feature Error Tolerances
The boundary lines for a deck/patio/porch etc. will have an error tolerance of 5cm/2”. This
means that if you have to adjust the location of the visible divider line by 5cm/2”, you do not
have to attribute it as an error towards the Drafter. Simply correct the line's location and move
on. If you have to adjust the line by anything greater than 5cm/2” then you must attribute a
level 1 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase
the tally by 1 per area instance.
If you have to alter the shape of the boundary as in converting a straight line to a curved line,
that counts as an error. There is no tolerance for an incorrect shape.
There is no tolerance for missing exterior features when they can be drafted.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L1 – incorrect shape of deck at scan 0003
b. L1 – Exterior area not drafted at scans 0004-0007
c. L1 (2) – missed exterior stairs at 0001 and 0028
d. L1 (4) – missed all exterior columns at 0003
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Missing Deck.
b. Deck is the wrong size.
c. Missed deck stairs
d. No columns
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several miniscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
