Door Errors & Omissions
A door error can be misplacement, misinterpretation, incorrectly sized, missing, incorrect
swing or incorrect type.
Doors must be:
●

Sized to the opening it exists in (inside of frame to inside of frame).

●

Sized based on the laser data if the visual data does not match.

●

Shown with the correct swing and type.

●

For ADS files (iGUIDE tasks with a black insight tag) doors need to be sized to 2”
○

Doors for ADS files are typically similar sizing and will not exhibit much variation.
Aim to demonstrate consistency with similar doors. Ex. Rare to see 24”, 23” and
25” doors in the same construction, they’re likely all 24” and the drafters sizing is
inaccurate.

○
*Hint: When it comes to doors, imagine yourself standing in front of it. How would the door
act/look if you were to pull or push on the handle.

Door Error Tolerances
Doors have an error tolerance of 5cm/2". This means that if you have to adjust the size of the
door by 5cm/2", you do not have to attribute it as an error towards the Drafter. Simply correct
the door and move on. If you have to adjust the door by anything greater than 5cm/2" then
you must attribute a level 1 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in
Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
There is no tolerance for an incorrect door swing and there is no tolerance for an incorrect
door type.
For iGUIDEs relating to ADS tasks, doors are required to be sized to 2” increments. Any
fractional sizing for doors on a task that has a black tag insight will result in an L1 error.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L1 – incorrect swing Q1 at 0001
b. L1 – incorrect orientation Q2 at 0003
c. L1 - incorrect door type at scan 002. Q2 from Q7
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Wrong door.
b. Missed doorway.
c. The door is wrong.
When noting the error, it is imperative to include the appropriate error level, a relevant scan,
and a brief, but succinct description of the error. It is also important to note what was
changed, such as Good Example Notes c. The “Bad Example” provides no details for the Drafter
and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several miniscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
