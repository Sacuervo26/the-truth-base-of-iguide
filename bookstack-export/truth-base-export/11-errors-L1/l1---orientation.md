Floor Orientation Errors & Omissions
An orientation error is when the floor has not been rotated to the correct position. Another
form of orientation error is when a floor has been marked as “independent” instead of
correctly copying and pasting a different floor's compass rotation value.

33. Floor plans will be drafted so that the front door is facing the bottom or righthand side of the iGUIDE Viewer. This ensures the front of the home will be facing the
bottom, right or left-hand side of the PDF floor plans.

Orientation Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance for orientation. If you have to rotate the floor(s) by 90,180 or 270 degrees
then you must assign a level 1 error towards the Drafter. The only time when orientation
becomes questionable is when there are two equally valid entry points within the home. In this
case it is a matter of preference and best choice. Additionally, only one error needs to be
attributed if the main floor is incorrectly oriented, but all other floors are oriented in relation to
the main floor. Example, main floor is incorrectly rotated 90 degrees clockwise, but 2nd and
Basement floor are oriented in relation to the main floor, this will only result in one L1 error,
not three for each floor.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L1 – incorrect orientation of property
b. L1 – incorrect orientation of Basement floor
c. L1 (2) – 2nd and Basement rotated 90* CCW to MF.
i.

Note – including the amount of rotation and direction can be beneficial
but typing out the error is time consuming. Using acronyms and short
hand can help to give detail while speeding up notetaking. In the example
above, it would read as “2nd and Basement floor 90 degrees CounterClockWise to Main Floor”

2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Rotated Main.
b. Reoriented the floors.
c. Front door is facing the wrong spot.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several miniscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
