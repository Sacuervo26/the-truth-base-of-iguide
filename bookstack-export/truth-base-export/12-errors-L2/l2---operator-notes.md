altogether, misinterpreting the notes, reading them but not fulfilling accommodable requests,
reading them and fulfilling unaccommodable requests.

scan names in the Project Explorer and the Drafter does not follow them.

Operator Notes Error Tolerances
When a room has a clear label as indicated with the Project Explorer and the Floor Plan does
not match it, then there is no tolerance except for the room labels (Primary & PC Baths).
Typically, operators will label a bedroom as “MASTER” and commonly a main floor bathroom as
“POWDER ROOM”. These two labels will be ignored as we label all Master Bedrooms as
“PRIMARY” and all bathrooms are labeled by Pieces. Aside from the allowable ignorable labels,
you must attribute a level 1 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in
Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
What labels can/should I ignore?
●

MASTER - LABEL PRIMARY INSTEAD

●

POWDER - LABEL BY PIECE COUNT INSTEAD

●

LANDING - LABEL HALL INSTEAD

Outside of operator defined labels that can be ignored, there is no tolerance and Drafters
must seek advice from their Shift or Regional Leader for clarification. Note down the error on
the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance (as in if the operator gave a list of
requests, any of them that could be fulfilled but wasn’t counted as an error per instance).
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L2 – Operator note to include garage not followed
b. L2 – Exterior dimensions request cannot be completed
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Missed operator note.
b. Note not followed.
c. Operator request unfulfilled.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
