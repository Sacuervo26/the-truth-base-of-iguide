Incorrect Drafting of Wall Types
An error regarding wall types is when the incorrect wall type has been used. Eg. A half wall was
drawn using a railing type line, or a full height interior wall was drawn using a half wall type.
Another situation where incorrect drafting of wall types can be seen is with split level
properties where walls are not marked shared interior between floors.

Wall Type & Size Error Tolerances
In terms of wall types, there is no tolerance. If you have to change the wall type then you must
attribute a level 2 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and
increase the tally by 1 per instance.
In terms of sizes, walls will have an error tolerance of 4cm/2”. This means that if you have to
adjust the size of the wall by 4cm/2” (2cm/1” per side of the wall) you do not have to attribute it
as an error towards the Drafter. Simply correct the minor thickness change and move on. If
you have to adjust the size of the wall by anything greater than 4cm/2” then you must attribute
a level 2 error towards the Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase
the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L2 – incorrect wall type for pass-thru @ 0004. D4 from D2
b. L2 – incorrect wall type stairs down near 022. D5 from D4

a. Wrong wall type.
b. Incorrect wall type for stairs.

Incorrect wall size.

Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
