Stair Length & Shape
A stair error regarding length is when a stair can clearly be drawn but gets stopped too early.
Another error is when the stairs do not start at the correct location.
A stair shape error is simply when a stair has not been drawn to the correct shape.
●

Eg. The stair is curved but gets drawn on an angle.

●

Eg2. The stair is actually “L” but it was cut short to be drawn as a straight stair.

Stair Length/Shape Error Tolerances
Stairs will have an error tolerance of 1” or 3cm in terms of its starting point, end point and
boundaries. If you have to adjust the starting or end point of the steps by anything greater
than 1”/3cm then you must attribute a level 2 error towards the Drafter. This also means that if
you have a curved stair and you adjust the curve by greater than 1”/3cm it also counts as an
error. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L2 - The stairs down from the 2nd floor should be extended by 2 extra steps as
seen by main floor pano #004.
b. L2 – 2nd floor stair down do not match MF stair up length
c. L2 - incorrect stair shape on stairs down from 2nd. U shape stairs drafted as L
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Stair wrong length.
b. Stairs too short.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
