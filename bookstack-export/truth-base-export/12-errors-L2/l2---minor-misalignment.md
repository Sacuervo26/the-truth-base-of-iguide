Minor Misalignment
A minor misalignment error is when a scan has not been placed reliably resulting in the laser
data to not completely overlap with other surrounding laser data. It may result in surrounding
walls or features being inaccurate. Eg. The minor misalignment caused an interior or exterior
wall to be 2 inches too thick. Some minor misalignment errors may include a scan that was not
aligned correctly but does not affect what has been drafted (rare case). Minor misalignment
errors can also be attributed to exterior scans that affect D1 wall thicknesses

Minor vs Major Misalignment
There are two different error types regarding misalignment of scans. Minor misalignment
generally does not affect the reported area of a property but instead will affect the reported
area of a room or rooms. Minor misalignment errors will often also affect wall thicknesses,
creating inconsistent walls that are not in-line, or with fluctuating width dimensions.
Furthermore, minor misalignment errors are generally smaller corrections, easier to identify
and correct. Major misalignment is an error that will generally affect the reported area of a
property. The size of the corrections for a major misalignment are extreme, such as rotating a
scan 90 to 180 degrees or total relocation of a scan on the floorplan.

Minor Alignment Error Tolerances
Scans have an error tolerance of 4cm/2”. This means that if you have to adjust the placement
of the scan by rotation, vertical or horizontal movement by 4cm/2”, you do not have to
attribute it as an error towards the Drafter as long as surrounding drafted elements are
unaffected. Simply correct the alignment and move on. If you have to adjust the scan by
anything greater than 4cm/2” then you must attribute a level 2 error towards the Drafter. Note
down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L2 (3) – Minor misaligned scan 0004-0006
b. L2 – Moved scan 0005 2” to the right
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Bedroom minorly misaligned.
b. Bathroom misaligned slightly.
c. Minor misalignment.
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
