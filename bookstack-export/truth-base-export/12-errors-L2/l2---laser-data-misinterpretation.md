Laser Data Misinterpretation
Laser data misinterpretation errors can come in the form of double laser lines. Sometimes the
laser captures the top of a lowered object as well as what’s behind it (think of the camera being
at a slight angle). The drafter then gets confused with which laser line to use.
●

Eg. In a walk-in closet the camera captures the exterior wall and the dresser in front of
the wall. If the drafter is not paying enough attention, they could draft the exterior wall
to where the laser data hits the dresser instead of the actual perimeter wall.

Another form of laser data misinterpretation is when it’s clear that only the laser data was
followed and the visuals were ignored. It’s important to use the visuals and laser data together
to determine drafted walls.
●

Eg. Often seen in kitchens, the laser data captures the upper kitchen cabinets instead of
the backsplash resulting in the wall being drawn to the cabinet instead of the
backsplash.

Laser Data Misinterpretation Error Tolerances
There is no tolerance as misinterpretation requires significant wall/line movement. If you have
to adjust walls due to misinterpretation then you must attribute a level 2 error towards the
Drafter. Note down the error on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L2 – Exterior walls drafted to upper cabinetry in kitchen at scans 0012, 0013
b. L2 – D2 walls drafted to incorrect laser data in WIC at 025. Laser hitting clothes,
not wall.
c. L2 – D1 walls in Rec Room at 001 drafted to ledge and not exterior walls.
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Kitchen Wall is wrong.
b. WIC wall, wrong spot.
c.

Laser hits the ledge, not the wall.

Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several minuscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
