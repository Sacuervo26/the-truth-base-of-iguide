Improper Restricted Heights, Ledges & Windows (room type)
Restricted Heights & Ledges
Restricted heights & ledges can fall into the category of “Missing a space that can be Drafted
Within the Boundary of the Property”. However, this error is more about improperly drawing
these types of spaces. An error involving a ledge or restricted height could be not drafting the
space thick enough.

Restricted Height/Ledge & Bay Window Error Tolerances
These instances will have an error tolerance of 1” or 3cm. This means that if you have to adjust
the boundary line of a restricted height, ledge or bay window line by 1” or 3cm, you do not
have to attribute it as an error towards the Drafter. Simply correct the window and move on. If
you have to adjust the boundary line of a restricted height, ledge or bay window line by greater
than 1” or 3cm then you must attribute a level 2 error towards the Drafter. Note down the
error on the notepad in Draft and increase the tally by 1 per area instance.
1. Good Example Notes:
a. L2 - Restricted height in Loft at scan 002 incorrectly drafted. Increased in size by
2”
b. L2 (3) - Basement perimeter ledge drafted undersized in Rec, Storage, 3PC.
Increased width of ledge by more than 1”
2. Bad Example Notes:
a. Restricted height wrong.
b. Ledge wrong.
c. Bay window wrong
Take a look at how much detail is provided for the “Good Example”. The location of the
problem/issue should be explained using the floor it is on, the pano/scans that can see it and
what room it’s contained in or next to. We must be descriptive by describing the issue and if
applicable, provide a solution or tips as to how to avoid it in future. The “Bad Example”
provides no details for the Drafter and is completely useless. Providing “Bad Example” notes
will not be tolerated.
*Note: The intent of the error tolerance is to ensure we are not overloading every Drafter with
several miniscule errors. This does not mean we do not correct the error in question. We
always correct everything whether it is within or outside of the error tolerance. The error
tolerance in this document is to be strictly adhered to, making up your own standards or
tolerances will not be permitted.
