Void Spaces 
Void spaces are a walled-off space that is inaccessible and takes up square footage on the floor 
plan. These areas show up as black on the floor plan. There are 2 ways to show these areas on 
the floor plan depending on the situation: 
 
● Draw the outline with a D2 line and label the space as room type VOID. This method 
takes away square footage from the room it is in and should be the first method used 
when drawing void spaces that are attached to an existing interior or exterior wall (use 
method II when it is too small to draw with lines or when the space is not attached to an 
exterior or interior wall).

● Place the square column object in the space of the void space. This method does not 
take away from the square footage of the room and should be used primarily for void 
spaces in the middle of a room not attached to a wall. Typically used wherever a 
column is located whether it is square (O2) or cylindrical (O1).

Excluded and Exterior Void Spaces 
Sometimes a void space needs to be drawn in spaces that are already excluded or are 
considered exterior. Excluded areas would be within the foundation perimeter such as areas 
with a restricted height. Exterior areas would be outside the foundation perimeter such as 
garages or a sun room with no heating. 
 
 
Figure 3 - Void Area selection 
 
● Determine if the area is Excluded or Exterior, and select the appropriate VOID from 
the dropdown menu. This void area will have a grey colour. 
● Next, right-click in the space and select Generate X. This will draw an X through the 
space, but on the Portal it will just show up as black just like other void spaces.
