KRMT – Ranch-Style Properties
What are Ranch-Style properties?
Ranch-Style homes are properties characterized by a mid-level entrance situated between two
primary floors. Upon entering, you are met with a small landing or foyer that leads directly to two
short flights of stairs.

Figure # 1 – Examples of Ranch-Style Entrance

From this landing, you transition immediately to either the Main Level or the Basement. Typically,
the main floor houses the kitchen, dining area, living room, and bedrooms, while the basement
level contains the recreation room, storage, and, in some cases, an integrated garage.

Drafting Ranch-Style Properties: The Two-Level Rule
Regardless of the floor configuration the operator submits the property with, after processing,
the property should only have two floors: the upper and lower levels.

1

Scenario #1: Two-Floor Submission
The operator submits a file with a two-floor configuration (e.g., Main Level and Basement), and
the foyer is already integrated into one of these floors:
•

Draft the property as-is.

•

Do not move scans to a different level.

•

Do not create a third level for the foyer area.

Figure # 2 – Two Floor Configuration

Scenario #2:
The operator submits the file with a three-floor configuration: the upper level, the lower level,
and the foyer area on its own floor.

2

Figure # 3 – Three Floor Configuration

Following the Two-Level Rule, the foyer area should be combined with one of the other two
floors. To decide which level should be used, click on the name of the foyer level to access its
properties. Check if the floor was submitted as above or below grade.
If the “Below grade (above below grade hint)” checkbox was not selected, all the scans currently
in the foyer level should be combined with the upper level of the property.

Figure # 4 – Combine with Upper Level

On the other hand, if the “Below grade (above below grade hint)” checkbox was selected, all the
scans currently in the foyer level should be combined with the lower level of the property.

Figure # 5 – Combine with Lower Level

After moving all the scans to the respective level, delete the empty floor.

Figure # 6 – Delete Upper Level

Once the levels are consolidated, proceed with drafting the property as normal.

Exception to the Two-Level Rule:
If - and only if - there are overlapping areas that can be drafted on both the Foyer Level and the
Upper Level, each floor should be drawn separately.

Important Note:
Only the Foyer Floor is being drafted using backsplit rules. Both the Upper and Lower levels should
be drawn normally to ensure the exterior wall is properly routed.
By drafting the full perimeter walls on the Upper Level, features like the window above the
entrance door can be accurately displayed. Similarly, on the Lower Level, any storage located
under the stairs can be properly represented where possible.
